La différence entre héritier et légataire

Guide: Succession après décès

Introduction

Though both have claims in a succession, heirs and legatees must not be confused. The article distinguishes between these two roles in inheritance matters.

Le légataire (The Legatee)

A legatee "receives all or part of the deceased's succession through a will." They obtain a specific bequest—an object or sum of money—and bear no responsibility for the succession's debts.

L'héritier (The Heir)

An heir "collects all or part of the deceased's succession by simple operation of law." Children and surviving spouses automatically inherit without requiring a testament. Importantly, "heirs are jointly liable for the succession's debts."

Dans le cas d'un testament (With a Will)

When a will exists, it designates beneficiaries called legatees. Three categories exist:

  • Légataires universels: Designated to receive the entire succession
  • Légataires à titre universel: Entitled to a share or category of assets
  • Légataires particuliers: Receive specific, determined property

The fundamental distinction: heirs inherit by law; legatees inherit by testamentary designation.
