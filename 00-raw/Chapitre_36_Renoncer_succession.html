Pourquoi et comment renoncer à une succession ?

Guide: Succession après décès

Les 3 options de la loi belge

Belgian law provides three options for heirs:

  • Accept the inheritance
  • Accept with benefit of inventory
  • Renounce the inheritance

Reasons to Refuse an Inheritance

1. Avoiding debts

Heirs can refuse to assume the deceased's debts. As noted, "lorsque l'actif de la succession dépasse le montant de l'actif successoral" (when liabilities exceed assets), renunciation becomes attractive.

2. Benefiting descendants

Refusing inheritance can allow direct transfer to the next generation, potentially saving on succession rights through what's called a "generation skip."

Costs of Renouncing an Inheritance

The pricing structure depends on inheritance value:

  • Below €6,093.20 (2023 threshold): Renunciation can be free at the notary
  • Above this threshold: Becomes payable; typically costs several hundred euros

Additional notary fees may apply for administrative tasks and inventory assessments.

Consequences of Renunciation

  • You lose heir status and receive no assets
  • Children automatically become heirs instead
  • Renunciation is final and typically irreversible
  • Minors require court approval to renounce

How to Renounce

Contact a notary of your choice to execute the renunciation document. Multiple heirs can sign together in one act.
