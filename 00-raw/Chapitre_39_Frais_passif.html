Déduire des frais au passif d'une succession

Guide: Succession après décès

What is Net Succession Assets?

The net succession value equals gross assets minus liabilities. Gross assets include all property, bank accounts, vehicles, and other holdings at death, while liabilities encompass all debts owed at that time.

Example provided: A deceased person with a €200,000 house and €10,000 bank account but €5,000 remaining loan would have a net succession of €205,000 on which succession taxes are calculated.

What Constitutes Succession Liabilities?

Liabilities represent all debts of the deceased—loans, unpaid invoices, and creditor obligations.

Deductible Expenses

Expenses can be added to liabilities if: "le défunt qui soit le redevable du frais...ET que le frais n'ait pas encore été payé" (the deceased is responsible AND the expense remained unpaid at death).

Exception: Funeral expenses are always deductible.

Acceptable items include: unpaid utilities, taxes, care facility fees, and outstanding loans.

Not acceptable: heir transportation costs, successor's professional fees, or succession taxes themselves.

Documentation Requirements

Heirs must provide invoices or creditor attestations to tax authorities with succession declarations. For pre-death expenses, proof of non-payment is required.

When It's Worth Deducting Expenses

Cost-benefit analysis matters—in typical successions with 5% taxation, small expenses may not justify the administrative effort required.
