Guide: Succession après décès

Drafting a succession declaration without a notary in Walloon Region and Brussels-Capital

The declaration must be submitted to SPF Finances within 4 months of death. While legally possible to complete independently, doing so carries significant risks including potential omissions, complexity, and exposure to penalties.

Steps to draft a succession declaration in Belgium without a notary

The article outlines 12 detailed steps, from downloading the official form to collecting information about the deceased, heirs, assets, and liabilities. Key sections address matrimonial regimes, testamentary provisions, donations, and usufruct mutations.

What self-declaring a succession entails

This requires:

  • Gathering all documentation about the deceased
  • Identifying all heirs
  • Listing all assets and liabilities
  • Completing complex administrative forms
  • Understanding matrimonial regime implications

Major drawbacks of self-declaration

"Manque d'exhaustivité," complexity without legal expertise, and risk of penalties for incorrect submissions are the main concerns.

The best alternative to a notary for drafting a succession declaration in Belgium

Using a succession bureau offers reduced costs (€1,000-5,000 savings versus notaries) while maintaining legal accuracy and reducing administrative burden.
