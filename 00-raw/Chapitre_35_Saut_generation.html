Le saut de génération

Guide: Succession après décès

Overview

This article explains how skipping a generation in inheritance works in Belgium, allowing grandparents' estates to pass directly to grandchildren rather than through their children.

How to Proceed

Children of the deceased must renounce their inheritance entirely, allowing grandchildren to inherit in their place. This requires filing a "declaration of renunciation" with a notary as an authenticated document, published in the Belgian Monitor within 15 days. The process is free if the estate's net assets are under €6,093.20.

After Skipping a Generation

The renouncing children lose heir status, making their children the legal heirs. This decision is irreversible and can reduce overall succession taxes since the estate is taxed only once instead of twice.

Limitations

The tax benefits vary by region:

  • Wallonia and Brussels: Currently, succession taxes for grandchildren cannot be less than what the renouncing parents would have paid
  • Flanders: Taxes are calculated based on each grandchild's inheritance share, enabling genuine tax savings

The article notes that as of January 1, 2024, Brussels plans to remove the "non-prejudice to tax administration" rule, potentially allowing greater savings.
